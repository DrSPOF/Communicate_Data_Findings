Data Visualisation GoFordBike Trip Data¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this slide deck I'd like to show the trends for 2 cities for a 12 month period for the GoFordBike trip data.
Trends I am having a look at are patterns relating to number of trips and durations of trips throughout the months, and do they allign to the expected weather trends by month and/or season. (Using a little bit of common sense re weather)

Dataset Overview¶

This workbook explores a dataset from GoFordBike for Boston and Washington for the period Dec 2019 - Nov 2020. The data set includes data relating to stations, dates, duration and city and has these details for a total of 4+ milion trips.

Number of trips by month for Boston and Washington¶

The month of April 2020 seems to be an outlier in the data. I was expecting to see lower results in the winter months vs the summer months. It is clear that Jun through to October show the higher numbers as expected. However, April is lower than both March and May, which I did not expect to see.

No data seems to be missing when performing further analysis. Without going into further detail, it looks like every day has less data. Doing a quick google search I can find some interesing information about the weather in Boston and Washington which might be the cause for the low numbers.

Duration of trip by month for Washington and Boston.¶

Does the trip duration increase in summer months? And is there a difference between the 2 cities?
Both cities seem to have longer durations for their trips in the spring and summer months (Assuming April-Jun is spring, and Jul-Sep is summer). Although the first Univariate showed low data sets for April, the duration looks to be inline with the expectation and the trend shown in the month before and after. Both cities show a similar seasonal trend as I had also expected.

Average trip duration by month and member type¶

Washington: All months clearly show longer durations for Casual users than Member users. This is what I expected to see, as I'd expect members to use the bike for a short commute, where casuals would use the bike to go on a leisurely bike ride.

Boston:Similarly to Washington, all months clearly show longer durations for Customer users than Subscriber users.

An interesting obersation is that the duration for bike rides in April for boh cities is the highest for both casuals/customers and members/subscribers. This is interesting to see given that we saw earlier that the numbers of rides were very low in April.

Also the duration for customers in December is on average over an hour. Which is intersting, assuming December is a reasonably cold month in Boston. Just to verify that result I had a look at the number of trips, to ensure only a few very long trips didn't distort the average for December. But it doesn't seem that December has an unexpected extremely low number of rides. It could be worth digging into this further and perhaps have a look at the individual durations for December for customers. (E.g. is there 1 or 2 rides that go over multiple days which would bring up the average duration for that partcilar month?)
